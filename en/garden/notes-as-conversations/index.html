Notes as conversations

A re-read note stops being a monologue. The one you wrote six months ago isn't quite you anymore: it corrects you, contradicts you, sometimes forgives you.

When I write a new note, I try to leave enough margin for the future-me to answer. I don't always succeed, but the intention changes the tone. You write with less hurry to wrap up.

Maybe that's why a garden isn't an archive. An archive stores; a garden speaks.
