Tending vs. publishing

Publishing and tending are two different verbs, and a blog confuses them on purpose. You publish once. You re-read if you feel like it. What counts toward the ego is the moment of pressing the button.

A garden inverts the economy. The point isn't to plant more, it's to water what you already planted. A note doesn't end when you upload it; it starts there. The good work happens on the visits after — when you re-read, trim, add, link.

The accounting changes too. Instead of "how many posts did I publish this year?", the question is "what have I been tending lately?". It's a metric that doesn't post well on LinkedIn, and that's probably why it's more honest.
