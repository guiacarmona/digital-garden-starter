The shape of attention

Attention isn't a flashlight. A flashlight lights what it points at and leaves the rest dark; attention, on the other hand, takes on the texture of its object.

If I spend an hour with a dense book, for the next day everything keeps arriving with that texture — dense, slow. If I spend that hour bouncing between apps, ideas come in app-shaped: short, noisy, hard to hold.

I don't yet know what to do with this observation. But I suspect it has practical consequences I'm not using.
